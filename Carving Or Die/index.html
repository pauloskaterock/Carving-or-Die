A piece of Skateboarding History.

Skateboarding emerged in the 1950s in the state of California, United States. The inventors of the sport were surfers, who, at low tide and few waves, put roller skates next to a wooden board. Thus, they created “asphalt surfing” or “sidewalk surfing”.

Skateboarding is practiced on streets around the world, or in specific places, such as skate parks. Freestyle – maneuvers performed in sequence, on the ground. Down Hill – descending slopes at the highest possible speed. Downhill Slide – descending slopes at high speed, performing skid maneuvers. Vertical - is practiced on the track, and can be subdivided into other modalities Street Style (street skateboarding) – in the most practiced modality around the world, benches, handrails, stairs in city streets are used as obstacles for skateboarding. Mini-ramps – practiced on a ramp that mixes the Street and Vertical styles

Skateboarding's most popular categories:


  • Vertical
  • Street
  • Down Hill
  • Freestyle
